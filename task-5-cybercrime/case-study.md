Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common types of cybercrime in India. It usually occurs when fraudsters trick users into sharing sensitive information 
such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or by clicking on malicious links.

A typical scenario involves a scammer calling or messaging the victim while pretending to be a bank official or a customer.
They may send a fake payment request or ask the user to approve a transaction. In some cases, they send phishing links that look like genuine banking websites. 
Once the user enters their details or approves the request, money is instantly deducted from their bank account.

Students and young users are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams.
The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in digital systems.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while making online payments and verifying all requests before taking any action.
